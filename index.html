What is a word square?

A word square is a block of words that reads the same, both across and down: 

        TEST
        ECHO
        SHOW
        TOWN

In a valid word square all words are the same length and the number of words equals the word length.

How do I play?

Click an empty cell on the game board and start typing.

Make sure that each word begins in the first column, but you may leave empty rows.

As you type, notice that your letters will appear in more than one cell. The trick to making a word square is finding words that contain the same letters where the words overlap.

Stick with shorter words at first. If you get stuck, press 'Search' and let the computer fill in the blanks. You may be surprised how many valid solution can be found, though some of the words may be very obscure.

How does this work?

The algorithm to build word squares identifies empty spaces and searches the english language for all words matching the pattern created by each word's intersection with the others.

A ternary search tree is used to provide ultra-fast pattern matching results. The search tree for this website can typically find all of the words matching a given pattern in less than 10 milliseconds!
